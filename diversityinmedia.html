The Last of Us - Season 1, Episode 3

In the midst of the zombie-apocalypse, two men void of intimacy find themselves engaging in a physical and emotional relationship. This representation of a masculine, male presenting gay couple on TV denouncing all heteronormative aspects found in the real world felt amazing to see. The episode itself has received rave reviews from The New York Times, The Washington Post, and Forbes.

Diversity and Inclusion in Media

Shows like Glee, Pose, Euphoria, Game of Thrones, The Last of Us, etc. have all featured characters and storylines that appeal to diverse demographics. This representation encompasses a spectrum of gender identities and a range of ethnicities.

Glee

The show ran for six seasons on FOX network. The cast was composed of characters with different sexual orientations and experiences with identity. One character that stands out is Unique played by Tony Award-winning performer Alex Newell! Newell joined the cast in 2010, season 3, where they debuted on stage showcasing their powerful vocals. This representation was significant because Newell's character displayed a young non-binary high school student navigating their identity as well as outside pressures like bullying and parental issues.

Pose

Nearest and dearest to the hearts of all its viewers, Pose took the world by storm with its release. The show follows the lives of transwomen and members of the LGBTQ+ community of color. The plot at the surface can be interpreted as the struggles people of color in the ballroom endure in the real world when they're embracing their identities unapologetically.
